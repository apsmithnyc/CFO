The Business Case for Enterprise Pharmacy Intelligence

Health systems are leaking millions of dollars every year because of inefficiencies, lack of adherence, and low capture volume. When it comes to pharmacy, many finance teams still don’t have real-time visibility. The processes and operations are not centralized and pharmacists are not trained to track the margins.

A study published in Health Affairs Scholar found that prior authorization accounts for $35 billion in annual U.S. healthcare administrative spending. Meanwhile, an AMA survey found that 82% of physicians report patients simply abandoning recommended treatment after running into authorization barriers.

As a result, pharmacy is simultaneously the largest uncontrolled cost and the fastest-growing margin opportunity. We are now seeing a new business case for systems of intelligence that are transforming pharmacy from a blind cost center to a significant revenue generator by serving more patients, growing the pharmacy operation, and capturing additional value without adding headcount.

Growth engine that diversifies revenue

“Now only 10% of my spend's inpatient, because I've built an enterprise that takes care of the patients when they walk out the door for treatment.”

DS Debbie Simonson Vice President, Chief Pharmacy Officer, Ochsner

Prior Authorization automation expands that top of the funnel, but that funnel narrows immediately if the organization cannot capture the patient when that PA clears. Payer lock out, patient choice, and LDD are all bottlenecks that hinder dispenses. Every PA that stalls, and every appeal isn’t tracked, is a dispense that happens somewhere else. Denied and delayed authorizations are revenue a system already earned and hasn’t collected. Pharmacy is doing all the PA work but capturing only a fraction, leaking millions in revenue to outside pharmacies.

  • 84% of administrative and clinical leaders cite financial pressures as their greatest threat
  • Hospital expenses are up 7.5% YoY and labor costs are rising at a permanently higher floor
  • 75% of hospital CFOs report higher cost pressure than previous years

Enterprise Pharmacy Intelligence flips that equation with an infrastructure to automate PA and also capture more volume across specialty, retail, infusion, and home delivery. This not only improves the patient experience but also diversifies revenue.
